How-To Toolkit

Follow along with foundational repair and maintenance tasks that every handy person should know.

Patch a Drywall Hole

Beginner
  1. Cut a square around the damaged area and remove loose drywall.
  2. Measure and cut a patch to fit snugly, securing it with backing strips.
  3. Apply joint compound and tape, feathering the edges before sanding smooth.

Replace a Faucet

Intermediate
  1. Shut off water supply lines and relieve pressure by opening the faucet.
  2. Disconnect supply hoses, remove mounting hardware, and lift out the old faucet.
  3. Install the new faucet, tighten fittings, and check for leaks once water is restored.

Wire a Light Switch

Advanced
  1. Turn off the circuit at the breaker and verify with a voltage tester.
  2. Remove the old switch, noting line, load, and ground conductors.
  3. Terminate the new switch according to wiring diagram, secure the box, and restore power.

Tune a Lawn Mower

Beginner
  1. Disconnect the spark plug and drain old fuel.
  2. Replace the air filter, spark plug, and sharpen the blade.
  3. Refill with fresh fuel, check oil level, and test-run the mower.

Winterize Outdoor Faucets

Intermediate
  1. Disconnect hoses and install insulated faucet covers.
  2. Shut off interior supply valves and open bleeder caps to drain water.
  3. Check exterior faucets for leaks and re-tighten caps after drainage.
